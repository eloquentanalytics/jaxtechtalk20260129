### Slide 5: Barrier #1 â€” The Scope Problem (2:00)

**Summary:** Enterprises naturally seek 80/20 solutions, but AI's transformative value only unlocks at 100% automation. Startups should target the most automatable subset of an enterprise's scope and serve customers who only need that slice at far lower prices. Enterprises should respond by identifying customer segments whose entire experience is automatable and building exclusively for them before expanding.

- Enterprises stop at 80-95% because diminishing returns make the last 5% look like a bad investmentâ€”but that final 5% is where the business model transforms
- Startups can pick only the process slices AI excels at; enterprises must run entire end-to-end processes they can't abandon
- The path forward is serving nonconsumption: customers below the "$50K floor" that incumbents rationally ignore

**Leverage Points (Scope Problem):**
- B1: The 95%â†’100% Threshold Is Non-Linear â€” the difference isn't 5%, it's a categorically different business

### Slide 6: Barrier #2 â€” The Shortest Path (2:00)

**Summary:** When AI delivers productivity gains, enterprises face a choice: cut costs (fire people) or invest in growth (do more with the same headcount). Cost cutting wins almost every timeâ€”not because leaders lack vision, but because it's the rational risk-adjusted choice. Growth is speculative and uncertain; headcount reduction delivers immediate, quantifiable savings that flow straight to the bottom line.

- Cost cutting delivers certain, immediate ROI that shows up in this quarter's earnings; growth investments are speculative bets that may take years to pay offâ€”or never
- Shareholders and analysts reward margin expansion; "we're investing in growth" sounds like "our margins are declining"
- The efficiency mindset (fewer people, same output) is linear; the volume mindset (same people, 100x output) requires imagining markets that don't exist yet

- D2: Percentage-Based Valuation Penalizes Lower Margins â€” Wall Street rewards margin expansion
- D5: Investment Horizon Mismatch â€” quarterly pressure favors immediate cost savings over long-term growth bets
- E2: Commission-Based Compensation Steers Toward Easy Wins â€” incentive structures optimize for current business
